Title: 3D Eye Tracking in Python
Date: 20250504
Description:
This video provides a brief overview of my open-sourced 3D eye tracker in Python. It's a heavily optimized port of the C++ repository released in 2017 by me, Yuta, Leszek, and Alex. Unlike the pupil fitter I released last year, this tracker provides the 3D origin and gaze direction for use with many eye tracking applications. 

Code: https://github.com/JEOresearch/EyeTracker/tree/main/3DTracker

Parts for camera and DIY glasses:
- GC0308 Eye Tracking Camera ($17): https://amzn.to/41x8p2W
- USB extension cables ($10): https://amzn.to/43SznVl
- Cheap sunglasses ($5) https://amzn.to/42KBLN3
- Pipe cleaners ($4) https://amzn.to/4jYwyXr

I recommend getting the sunglasses and pipe cleaners from the dollar store, but using the links above do help support the channel ;) 

In my next few videos, I will try to provide instructions for getting the tracker working as a gaze cursor for a monitor or AR/VR headset. 

For anyone concerned about IR light exposure, I measured these cameras at 3cm away. For consistency/reference, I also measured two other light sources using the same meter: 

- GC0803 @ 3cm:  3.6 W/m²  (0.36 mW/cm²)
- Meta quest pro eye tracker @ lens depth:  2.2 W/m²  (0.22 mW/cm²)
- Ambient light in my hallway (7PM, evening sunlight on floor): 1.3 W/m²  (0.13 mW/cm²)

Note that this is a first release, and I will continue to optimize as time goes on. Please post any questions in the comments or on the Discord.
