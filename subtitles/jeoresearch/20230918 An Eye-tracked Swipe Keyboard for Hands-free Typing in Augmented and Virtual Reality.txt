Title: An Eye-tracked Swipe Keyboard for Hands-free Typing in Augmented and Virtual Reality
Date: 20230918
Description:
This is a demonstration of the work being done at Augusta University to help physically impaired individuals or people who cannot use their hands to type. We combine eye tracking with the concept of a swipe keyboard, which is rendered in video see-through augmented reality (AR). This video was recorded through the Varjo XR-3, which is an AR display that does eye tracking and lets you see the environment. Two virtual eyes are rendered at the beginning of the video to show what the user's eye movements look like. 

The citation for this work is as follows: 
Mansour, A. and Orlosky, J. (2023) Approximated Match Swiping: Exploring more Ergonomic Gaze-based Text Input for XR. To appear in the adjunct proceedings of the International Symposium on Mixed and Augmented Reality. IEEE.
